History

Merida is the capital and largest city of the state of Yucatan in Mexico. It was founded in january 6th 1542 over an ancient mayan city by Francisco de Montejo, a spanish conqueror. This ancient geographycal situation has led to a blend of cultures of spanishs and mayan which is palpable in architecture, cuisine, traditions, language. Mote information can be found here

Points of interest

  • Paseo de montejo: The main avenue of the city with outstanding foreign architecture.
  • Casa de los montejo: Family residence of the montejo during the colonial epoch. It is now a museum.
  • Cenotes: Natural swimming holes formed in limestone caves, found throughout the Yucatan peninsula.

Some of my favorite dishes

  • Cochinita pibil: A sunday classic un Mérida. Roasted pork marinated in achiote paste and served as tacos or sandwiches.
  • Chicken breading: Author's absolute favorite dish. Fried breaded chicken served with green spaguetti (really important).
  • Chicken Poc Chuc: Good healthy option. While this dish uses pork as a more popular way to serve it, chicken is a healthier optin due to its low fat and high protein condition.
